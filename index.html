Survey Statistics & Causal Inference Collaboration

What happens when survey methodologists and causal inference researchers work together? I'm interested in how design-based thinking and potential-outcomes frameworks can inform each other, and what a unified error framework might look like.

  • paper Toward a Total Causal Error Framework — Observational Studies (forthcoming)
🤝

Statistician & Evaluator Collaboration

Statisticians and program evaluators often speak different languages even when working on the same problem. I'm interested in how we build better bridges through shared vocabulary, collaboration structures, and professional norms.

  • webinar The Evidence Partnership: Bridging Statistical Thinking and Evaluation Practice July 2025, Evidence to Impact Network
  • article Panelists Discuss How Statisticians, Evaluators Turn Evidence into Impact March 2025, Amstat News
  • slides Introductory Overview Lecture: Program Evaluation August 2024, Joint Statistical Meetings
  • slides A Retrospective Evaluation of a COVID-era Community Engagement Program August 2023, Joint Statistical Meetings
🔬

Study Design & Sample Size Determination

A lot of the real inferential action happens before any data are collected. I'm working on clearer frameworks for power analysis and sample size in complex and observational settings.

  • webinar The Power of Power: Elevating Power Analysis as a Tool for Credible, Fundable, and Impactful Research August 2025, Royal Statistical Society Emerging Applications Section
  • slides Sample Size Determination in Survey Design: Balancing Cost, Precision, and Bias February 2026, AAPOR Survey Costs Workshop
🪶

Statistics in the Field: Evaluating Conservation Programs

Not all data collection happens in a lab or a survey; some of it happens on farms in New Jersey and Pennsylvania, checking nest boxes for kestrel eggs. Working with my dad on an American Kestrel conservation program got me thinking about how we make evaluation decisions in the field: what to measure, what counts as success, and how to balance statistical rigor with the realities of working in nature. I'm interested in what citizen science and ecological monitoring can teach statisticians about study design under constraints.

  • report Summary of an American Kestrel Nest Box Program in New Jersey and Pennsylvania Steve C. Eisenhauer & Elizabeth W. Eisenhauer · Pennsylvania Birds, 2022
🏛️

Inspired by History

The history of statistics is full of people who were rigorous, courageous, and largely forgotten, and I think there's a lot we can learn from them about what statistical practice can and should be. I'm interested in how historical figures and moments can inform how we think about methodology, professional norms, and how best to shape the discipline.

  • webinar Kate Claghorn: Progressive Era Statistician, First Woman ASA Fellow May 2026, International Statistical Institute Committee on Women in Statistics & Caucus for Women in Statistics and Data Science & American Statistical Association History of Statistics Special Interest Group
